#### 糸¹

{絲} \*sə "thread" & {幺} \*ʔˤiw "tiny" & {絃} \*qʰʷˤins "rope"

Depiction of a bundle of threads (knots absent or present only in the
bottom part). Initially it was used for words {絲}, {幺} and {絃}. Later
it began to be used only for {絲}.

- 季旭昇 2014 - 說文新證 \[2nd ed.\] (887)
- 李守奎 & 肖攀 2015 -
  清華簡《系年》文字考釋與構形研究・幺、玄、糸、𢆶、絲等相關字考

**Forms:**

纟 - Shortening based on the cursive form in the left part of compound
glyphs. Modern simplified form in China.

- Bökset R. 2021 - Long Story of Short Forms: Simplified Chinese
  Characters from A to Z (278-279)

#### 糸²

\*{糸} \*mˤek "silk thread"

Shortening of 絲. Created for *Shuowen Jiezi* to differentiate 糸 from
絲.

- 田煒 2016 - 西周金文字詞關係研究 (35-43)
